People with Dementia

What are they saying

Still Evelyn

Evelyn talks about living with a dementia and how she continues to enjoy life.

Martin

Martin lives in Belfast. He has four sons and seven grandchildren who live nearby. He enjoys snooker, watching football and is an avid Manchester United fan.

Before Diagnosis

Before he was diagnosed, Martin noticed he was putting money and remote controls in the rubbish bin. He knew this was unusual. Having had a family member with dementia he recognised the signs and symptoms. Martin went to his GP for help.

Danny's Story

Danny talks about being diagnosed with a dementia and how he continues to enjoy life. Danny had little knowledge of dementia before his diagnosis. He hadn’t considered dementia could be the reason for the symptoms he was experiencing. But like many people with a dementia diagnosis, Danny was glad he had an explanation for the changes he was experiencing, rather than worrying about what else it might be.

Frances

I was very aware of dementia before my diagnosis as my mother and grandmother had dementia.

Leading an active life

I want to show other people that there is life after a dementia diagnosis. I am living proof of that! I want people to know that leading an active life is much better than staying still. If you’re sitting around all day doing nothing, all you can do is ponder your diagnosis.

Seamus

Diagnosed with posterior cortical atrophy

About four years ago, I had an idea that all wasn’t well with my memory. I had different tests and scans. Two weeks after a memory test, I was diagnosed with posterior cortical atrophy. This is a rare type of Alzheimer’s disease. I was given a course of medication

Talking to family and friends about diagnosis

I would encourage people to live as normal a life as you can, get out and about as much as possible. There are lots of dementia support groups, such as Dementia NI. Don’t hide your diagnosis, talk to your family and friends about it.
